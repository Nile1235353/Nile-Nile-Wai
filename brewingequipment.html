Espresso Machine

Price: $150 - $500 (depending on size and material).
Description: Espresso machines use high pressure to force hot water through finely ground coffee beans, creating a concentrated shot of espresso.
Usage Tips: Grind size: Use a fine grind for espresso.
Tamperessure: Apply even pressure when tamping the coffee grounds.
Water temperature: Use hot water, but not boiling, for optimal extraction.
Extraction time: Aim for a shot time of 20-30 seconds.

Pour-Over Kettle

Price: $150 - $500 (depending on size and material).
Description: A kettle designed for pouring hot water over coffee grounds in a pour-over cone.
Usage Tips: Water temperature: Use water that is just off the boil (around 200°F or 93°C).
Pouring technique: Pour the water in a circular motion, ensuring even saturation of the grounds.
Grind size: Use a medium-coarse grind for pour-over.
Bloom: Allow the grounds to bloom for 30 seconds before pouring the rest of the water.

French Press

Price: $150 - $500 (depending on size and material).
Description: A coffee maker that uses a plunger to press the coffee grounds down, separating the brewed coffee from the grounds.
Usage Tips: Grind size: Use a coarse grind for French press.
Steeping time: Steep the coffee grounds for 3-4 minutes.
Plunging: Press the plunger slowly and evenly to avoid disturbing the grounds.
Cleaning: Clean the French press immediately after use to prevent coffee residue buildup.

Cold Brew Maker

Price: $150 - $500 (depending on size and material).
